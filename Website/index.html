Why

Often we get together for dinner parties and find ourselves trying to find something to play. It's always a bit of a struggle getting the right kind of game for each group dynamic because we never invite the same set of people over and over.

We're always looking for games that involve some sort of strong cooperation with a competitive element to it. Games that fall under this category (Charades, Pictionary, Fishbowl, Literature, etc) are usually over played and very tiring, especially for us as hosts. At the same time, board games take a while for us to setup and explain, but more importantly, not everyone likes them.

In an effort to expand our selection of post-dinner activities, we thought why not start creating the games we wished existed? And thus, resync was born.

What

Multiplayer video games can integrate amazing technology leading to intricate team dynamics but often miss out on that personable interaction. On the other hand, board games are great for interacting with other people but as the complexity of a game increases, so does the amount of time it takes to learn and understand the game. We personally really enjoy board games that err on the complicated side, but struggle to find people to play with us.

At the same time, we generally find that both video games and board games tend to struggle when you get to medium-sized group dynamics so akin to our dinner parties. When you've got more than 6 to 8 people to entertain, you want a game that's simple to understand yet still riveting to play.

This is what we're trying to achieve with resync. We believe that a good balance between video games and board games can blend the best of both worlds. We're trying to make games for the average person who likes video games and likes board games, but isn't a die hard fan for one over the other.
